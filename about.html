Our Story

Where it all started

Rera's Treat is a taste of mother's kitchen and love, wrapped up in finger foods for you and yours.

The name "Rera" isn't a word you'll find anywhere else — it was coined, built from scratch, just for this. Because some things you're building deserve a name that's never belonged to anyone before you.

That's the same spirit behind everything that leaves our kitchen in Ogijo. Every spring roll, every skewer, every tray of smallchops is made the way a mother makes food for people she loves — with patience, with care, and without cutting corners just because no one's watching.

We started small, the way most good things do: a few trays, a few orders, a lot of word-of-mouth. Today, Rera's Treat shows up at birthdays, owambes, quiet Sunday get-togethers, and the occasional "I just wanted something to snack on" order — because finger foods aren't just party food. They're an excuse to gather, to share, to pass a plate around and let everyone grab a piece. From Ogijo to Itaoluwo, Shimawa, Odonguyan, and Lukosi, our finger foods, small chops, pasta, and drinks show up wherever people are gathering.

Whether you're feeding fifty guests or just yourself on a good day, our promise stays the same: fresh, made-to-order, and finished with the kind of love you'd expect from home.
